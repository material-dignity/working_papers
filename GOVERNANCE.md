# Project Governance: Systemic Dignity Infrastructure (SDI)

This document establishes the official operational, administrative, and strategic governance model for the Systemic Dignity Infrastructure (SDI) open-source framework. 

To preserve the intellectual integrity and analytical focus of the research, this project enforces a strict, permanent separation between **Systems Architecture (Research & Development)** and **Executive Operations (Capital Procurement, Media, and Pilot Implementation)**.

---

## 1. Core Organizational Structure

The SDI project operates under a split-authority architecture to ensure the model remains auditable, falsifiable, and protected from organizational capture.

```
┌──────────────────────────────┐
│  Principal Systems Architect │ (Technical Direction & Validation)
└──────────────┬───────────────┘
               │ (Strict Operational Separation)
               ▼
┌──────────────────────────────┐
│      Executive Director      │ (Vacant / Interim Administration)
└──────────────┬───────────────┘
               │
┌──────────────┴───────────────┐
▼                              ▼
┌──────────────────┐    ┌──────────────────┐
│ Philanthropic &  │    │ Municipal Pilot  │
│ Capital Partners │    │ Operatives       │
└──────────────────┘    └──────────────────┘
```

### A. The Office of the Principal Systems Architect
* **Scope**: Retains ultimate authority over the engineering core, technical whitepapers, falsifiable clause criteria, and algorithmic resource frameworks.
* **Public Interface**: The Principal Architect does **not** serve as a spokesperson, does **not** conduct public or media interviews, and does **not** interface directly with funding entities or their representatives. 
* **Communication**: All technical directives are committed directly to this repository via verified cryptographic Git signatures under administrative project aliases.

### B. The Office of the Executive Director (Current Status: VACANT)
* **Scope**: Serves as the sole public-facing legal and administrative entity of the SDI framework. This office is responsible for fiscal tracking, corporate partnership negotiations, and municipal pilot execution.
* **Media & Funding Representation**: The Executive Director (once appointed via institutional sponsorship) serves as the singular public spokesperson for the project. 
* **Direct Mandate**: The Executive Director is legally bound by non-disclosure protocols to shield the personal communication channels and private identity of the Principal Systems Architect.

## 2. Decision-Making Framework

To maintain the project’s standard as a falsifiable engineering specification rather than a political initiative, decisions are stratified into clear vectors:

| Decision Type | Governing Entity | Process |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Technical Core Updates** | Principal Systems Architect | Unilateral integration based on empirical verification and data validation. |
| **Capital Allocation & Grants** | Executive Director & Fiscal Sponsor | Subject to audit against the `PILOT-BUDGET.md` specifications. |
| **Pilot Implementation Adjustments** | Municipal Pilot Team & Executive Director | Sub-routine deployment; must not violate the core Dignity Stack baseline. |

---

## 3. Communication & Procurement Protocols

All external entities, including high-net-worth entrepreneurial funding teams, institutional foundations, and municipal governments, must communicate strictly through the channels designated below.

### A. Philanthropic & Capital Inquiries
* Direct interactions with the development team are prohibited. 
* Capital proposals must be forwarded to the project's administrative proxy contact point or designated fiscal sponsor entity.
* Funding will only be accepted via structured institutional pathways (e.g., Blind Trusts, Family Office allocations, or Fiscal Sponsorship non-profit structures) that isolate the technical research team from financial management.

### B. Media and Public Relations
* The research team does not participate in press conferences, podcasts, or journalistic interviews.
* Media entities may cite the whitepapers and repository contents under the terms of the project's open-source license, but requests for personal commentary will be systematically routed to an automated repository responder.

---

## 4. Amendments to Governance

Any modification to this governance document that compromises the anonymity, operational isolation, or veto authority of the Principal Systems Architect over the technical core will immediately falsify the operational branch of the repository, triggering an automatic migration of the technical core to a separate, isolated organizational node.
