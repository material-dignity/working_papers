# 02. CAPITAL STACK AND YIELD MECHANICS
## Material Dignity Infrastructure (MDI) Prototype

### 1. OVERVIEW OF REQUIRED CAPITALIZATION
Executing the One California Plaza prototype requires $220,000,000 in total capital. This figure encompasses acquisition costs, facility adaptation, Day One stabilization reserves, and thirty-six months of capitalized interest. The capitalization structure explicitly bridges the gap between unproven operational theory and institutionally deployable financial instruments.

### 2. TRANCHE A: CATALYTIC FIRST-LOSS EQUITY
*   **Total Volume:** $88,000,000 (40% of Capital Stack)
*   **Target Sources:** Mission-driven family offices, philanthropic endowments
*   **Instrument:** Program-Related Investments (PRIs)
*   **Target Yield:** 4% (Deferred)
*   **Structural Function:** Tranche A absorbs initial operational volatility during the facility stabilization phase. By accepting a subordinated position and a deferred yield, this capital de-risks the senior debt, effectively purchasing the required financial validation data.

### 3. TRANCHE B: SENIOR CONCESSIONARY DEBT
*   **Total Volume:** $132,000,000 (60% of Capital Stack)
*   **Target Sources:** Community Development Financial Institutions (CDFIs), mission-aligned commercial lenders
*   **Target Yield:** 8% (Current)
*   **Structural Function:** Tranche B operates as traditional commercial real estate debt. It is strictly secured against the physical asset (One California Plaza), acquired significantly below replacement cost. This tranche requires no philanthropic mandate; it responds entirely to standard commercial risk-adjusted return metrics.

### 4. USE OF PROCEEDS
1.  **Distressed Asset Acquisition:** Purchasing physical commercial real estate at sub-replacement valuations.
2.  **Facility Adaptation:** Converting commercial floorplates into biologically stabilized relational housing pods.
3.  **Capitalized Interest Reserve:** $10,560,000 allocated explicitly to cover Tranche B debt service during the primary thirty-six-month operational stabilization phase.
4.  **Operational Stabilization Fund:** Funding initial clinical and navigational staffing prior to full sovereign revenue realization.

### 5. LIQUIDITY EVENT
Following the Day 365 operational audit validating stable CalAIM revenue and $50,000 per capita avoided costs, traditional institutional capital (pension funds, infrastructure funds) will refinance the entire stack. This liquidity event repays Tranche B completely and permits Tranche A to cycle capital into subsequent replication projects.
