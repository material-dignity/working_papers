# Policy Comparison Data: Executive Order 14321 and STREETS Program

## Executive Order 14321
* **Title**: Ending Crime and Disorder on America's Streets
* **Signing Date**: July 24, 2025
* **Core Mandate**: Directs federal agencies to shift grant funding toward jurisdictions that enforce bans on urban camping, loitering, and public drug use.
* **Clinical Focus**: Expands civil commitments and assisted outpatient treatment (AOT) pathways for individuals with high-acuity behavioral health disorders.
* **Alignment**: Explicitly de-prioritizes traditional Housing First models and harm-reduction services in favor of treatment-mandated programs.

## STREETS Program
* **Acronym**: Safety Through Recovery, Engagement, and Evidence-based Treatment and Supports
* **HHS Secretary**: Robert F. Kennedy Jr.
* **Announced Funding**: 96 million dollars over four years announced in June 2026, as part of a 700 million dollar behavioral health package.
* **Structure**: Grants of up to 3 million dollars per year awarded to eight pilot communities.
* **Grants Policy**: Program rules restrict funding from going to Housing First or harm-reduction services, requiring instead a focus on sobriety, recovery, and clinical integration.

## Y-Foundation Retention Baselines
* **National Model**: Finland Housing First national program
* **Key Provider**: Y-Foundation
* **Empirical Rate**: 80% to 85% housing retention for long-term chronically homeless cohorts with complex health needs.
* **System Philosophy**: Unconditional, immediate permanent housing placements with integrated on-site support services, replacing the staircase model.
