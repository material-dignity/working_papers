# Systemic Dignity Infrastructure: Core Taxonomy Glossary

- **Agentive Selfhood**: The behavioral capacity to initiate unprompted action, participate in governance, and articulate long-term economic goals. Recovered through internal micro-economic participation rather than external job placement.
- **Depreciating Asset Trap**: The condition experienced by hidden/vehicular homeless populations where partial identity capital is consumed entirely by the maintenance and legal defense of a rapidly depreciating mobile shelter.
- **Dunbar Cohort**: A social grouping scaled to human cognitive limits (approximately 150 individuals, or smaller operational sub-pods of 12-15) used to architect relational dignity and mutual accountability without triggering nervous system hyper-vigilance.
- **Identity Capital**: The intangible psychological currency, comprising self-efficacy and a coherent professional narrative, required for successful labor market navigation. Parallel to the clinical concept of "recovery capital."
- **Organic Vernacular Pedagogy (OVP)**: A pedagogical methodology that translates complex systemic and structural failure theories into accessible, body-first language anchored in direct lived experience, explicitly rejecting academic credentialism.
- **Phase Zero**: The preliminary stage of metabolic and biological stabilization required before any economic, behavioral, or psychological demands are placed on an individual experiencing chronic unsheltered collapse.
- **Return Deficit**: A socioeconomic friction mechanism where standard job-seeking efforts yield severely diminished returns due to stigmatized institutional histories (e.g., carceral records, gaps in employment, evictions).
- **Tenancy Bridge Guarantee**: A self-executing contractual mechanism bridging Phase One stabilization (the MDI tower) and Phase Two sovereign tenancy by leveraging clinical revenue (e.g., managed care billing) to guarantee external master leases.
