## Supplemental Draft Insertion: Asset Arbitrage and Skyscraper Adaptation

**Target: WP6 (Political Economy)**

### Automated Office-to-Pod Conversion (AOPC)
The macroeconomic leverage of the MDI framework relies on the adaptive reuse of distressed Class B and C downtown office towers. Rather than engaging in custom, ground-up development, MDI deploys standardized, slide-in structural inserts. These units contain pre-engineered plumbing, STC-65 acoustic partitions, and HVAC ties that hook into a skyscraper's core mechanical shaft. 

### Commercial Valuation Arbitrage
The post-COVID remote work transition drove vacancy rates across Class B and C downtown inventory above thirty percent by mid-decade. Building owners are left servicing commercial mortgages on properties generating insufficient occupancy revenue. This structural market failure provides the acquisition mechanism for MDI. 

By purchasing distressed towers at an average of seventy-one dollars per square foot against a replacement construction cost of four hundred dollars per square foot, the system executes a massive capital arbitrage. The STC-65 acoustic partition work, the Phase Zero decompression floor, and the Dunbar Pod residential conversion add an estimated forty-three dollars per square foot. The total all-in acquisition and conversion cost amounts to one hundred fourteen dollars per square foot. Constructing an equivalent building from bare ground would require six times that capital expenditure.
