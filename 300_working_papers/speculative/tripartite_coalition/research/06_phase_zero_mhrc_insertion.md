## Supplemental Draft Insertion: Phase Zero and Sub-Acute Clinical Billing

**Target: WP7 (Tripartite Coalition Architecture)**

### MHRC Licensing and CalAIM Integration
The financial viability of the MDI framework bypasses local municipal housing budgets by integrating directly with state and federal healthcare streams. This is achieved by licensing the Dunbar Pod floors and Phase Zero triage units as Mental Health Rehabilitation Centers (MHRCs). 

By establishing the architecture as a clinical intervention environment rather than a traditional shelter, the facility is legally authorized to charge its operations to Medi-Cal sub-acute clinical billing. 

### Federal Matching and Fiscal Stability
Under the CalAIM framework, the state draws down fifty percent federal matching funds through Medicaid to cover the costs of these MHRC sub-acute beds. The system funds residential stabilization entirely through healthcare budgets. By treating sleep deprivation, metabolic collapse, and neuro-chemical instability as treatable biological deficits requiring specialized architectural containment (STC-65 soundproofing, secure perimeters), the operational overhead is underwritten by the federal government's healthcare apparatus. Outcomes are then tracked via objective bio-metric stabilization and housing-retention metrics to ensure continuous compliance and funding alignment.
