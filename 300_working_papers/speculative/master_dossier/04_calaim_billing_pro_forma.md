# 04. SOVEREIGN REVENUE MECHANICS
## Material Dignity Infrastructure (MDI) Prototype

### 1. REVENUE THESIS
Operational sustainability for the MDI prototype relies entirely upon sovereign state funding via Medicare and Medicaid (Medi-Cal). The model rejects philanthropic grants and municipal bonds for ongoing operations. Revenue generation depends entirely upon the California Advancing and Innovating Medi-Cal (CalAIM) Community Supports framework.

### 2. BILLING ARCHITECTURE
The California Public Benefit Corporation (PBC) bills managed care plans directly for services rendered to the stabilized population. The target population (chronically unsheltered individuals) universally qualifies for maximum CalAIM intervention tiers due to advanced metabolic degradation and severe comorbid conditions.

**Core HCPCS Billing Codes:**
*   **H0043 U6 (Housing Transition Navigation Services):** Funds the initial intake, stabilization mapping, and logistical coordination required to transition individuals from street environments into the MDI facility.
*   **H0044 U2 (Housing Deposits):** Funds initial capital requirements including sanitation, essential furnishings, and baseline environmental stabilization needs.
*   **T2050 U6 (Housing Tenancy and Sustaining Services):** Provides ongoing, permanent monthly revenue funding relational support, conflict resolution, and behavioral stabilization protocols ensuring long-term tenancy.
*   **T2033 U6 (Recuperative Care / Medical Respite):** Provides premium reimbursement rates for individuals requiring acute stabilization following discharge from emergency medical networks, explicitly preventing street-level readmission.

### 3. THE AVOIDED COST METRIC
Los Angeles County currently expends approximately $50,000 annually per unsheltered individual, primarily through reactive emergency medical services, police interventions, and specialized sanitation sweeps. 

The MDI prototype captures this value through CalAIM billing. By stabilizing the individual within One California Plaza, the PBC eliminates the erratic emergency room utilization. The state redirects a portion of the $50,000 avoided cost into the CalAIM billing channels, funding the facility operations permanently.

### 4. OPERATIONAL PRO FORMA
Sovereign billing initiates immediately upon resident intake. The $10.56M capitalized interest reserve covers Tranche B debt service while the PBC scales billing operations across the initial 1,000-resident cohort. Full operational break-even is projected at Day 270, with stabilized positive cash flow generated exclusively through sovereign reimbursement channels by Day 365.
