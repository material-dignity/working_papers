## Supplemental Draft Insertion: System Evaluation Matrix

**Target: 999 (Final Documentation / Master Dossier)**

### Comparative System Matrix
Evaluating alternative infrastructure models across speed, cost, regulatory feasibility, and long-term stability demonstrates the systemic completeness of the Material Dignity Infrastructure (MDI) framework over competing models.

### Alternative Frameworks
1.  **Autonomous Mobile Fleet Deployment (AMFD)**: Deploys heavy-duty industrial vehicles with self-contained ALMU pods to hook into municipal grids at transit nodes. Relocates based on real-time demographic demand.
2.  **Tax-Credit Decentralized Micro-Zoning (TDMZ)**: Distributes micro-pods to single-family backyards via federal tax credits, using mobile medical units for care delivery.
3.  **Automated Office-to-Pod Conversion (AOPC - Standalone)**: Rapidly retrofits commercial towers but lacks MDI's demographic sorting and metabolic stabilization clinical systems.
4.  **Universal Infrastructure Vouchers (UBIV)**: Issues blockchain-verified vouchers funded by Medicaid matching to let individuals rent private hygiene and sleep pods.

### Failure Analysis of Competing Models
*   **Standalone AOPC** fails because it lacks the Phase Zero metabolic stabilization layer. Placing traumatized brains into independent apartments without addressing their damaged biology results in high eviction rates and behavioral decompensation.
*   **AMFD** provides deployment speed but faces high municipal opposition regarding zoning and fails to provide long-term residential stability.
*   **TDMZ** encounters local zoning battles (NIMBYism) and lacks the clinical density required for efficient medical delivery.
*   **MDI Wins** because it synchronizes physical skyscraper conversions (cost efficiency) with biological triage (Phase Zero) and localized peer governance (Dunbar Pods), treating human biology and urban architecture as an integrated system.
