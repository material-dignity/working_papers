# Field Manual 1: Autonomic State Identification and Routing Matrix

## Operational Baseline
The field worker does not evaluate behavior; the field worker evaluates autonomic nervous system state. Behavior is the output of the state. Engaging a dorsal-vagal shutdown state with a ventral-vagal script (asking questions, expecting eye contact) triggers immediate biological threat detection and ensures intake failure.

This matrix dictates the mandatory physical approach and verbal script based exclusively on the observable biological state of the target individual at the moment of contact.

---

## 1. The Identification Matrix

| Autonomic State | Observable Biological Indicators | Threat Perception |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **State 1: Dorsal Vagal (Shutdown)** | Total immobility, slumped posture, shallow or imperceptible breathing, flat affect, vacant gaze, dissociation from immediate environment. | Extreme or inescapable. Organism feigns death to conserve metabolic energy. |
| **State 2: Sympathetic (Mobilized)** | Pacing, rigid musculature, rapid chest-level breathing, hyper-vigilant scanning, accelerated speech, sweating. | Active and immediate. Organism floods with adrenaline, preparing to fight or flee. |
| **State 3: Ventral Vagal (Engaged)** | Relaxed posture, deep belly breathing, spontaneous eye contact, tonal variation in voice, facial micro-expressions. | Safe. Organism is capable of social engagement and prefrontal cognitive processing. |

---

## 2. The Execution Matrix

### State 1: Dorsal Vagal (Shutdown) Protocol
*   **Approach Vector:** Approach on an oblique angle. Do not approach head-on. Maintain minimum six-foot distance. **Forbidden:** Direct, sustained eye contact (perceivable as predation). 
*   **Physical Posture:** Lower center of gravity. Sit or crouch if the individual is on the ground. Keep hands visible and still.
*   **Script Constraint:** **Forbidden:** Interrogative questions (e.g., "How are you?", "Do you want to come inside?"). The prefrontal cortex is offline. Questions demand processing capacity that does not exist.
*   **Mandatory Script:** Declarative statements of safety and presence only. "I am going to leave this water here." "I am going to sit here for two minutes, and then I will leave." 
*   **Operational Routing:** Deliver material supply without demanding acknowledgment. Log the contact. Withdraw. Repeat daily until autonomic shift to State 2 or State 3 occurs.

### State 2: Sympathetic (Mobilized) Protocol
*   **Approach Vector:** Maintain distance. Never block an exit path. Position parallel to the individual, facing the same direction, rather than face-to-face.
*   **Physical Posture:** Standing, relaxed, hands open and visible. Slow all physical movements. 
*   **Script Constraint:** **Forbidden:** Commands to "calm down" or attempts to logically argue. 
*   **Mandatory Script:** Match the intensity but lower the pitch. Use concrete, short sentences. Validate the threat perception without confirming delusion. "You are moving fast. I am staying right here. You have space."
*   **Operational Routing:** Allow the adrenaline cycle to complete. Do not force the intake offer. Wait for physiological deceleration (slower breathing, stopping pacing) before introducing logistical choices.

### State 3: Ventral Vagal (Engaged) Protocol
*   **Approach Vector:** Direct approach. Face-to-face orientation is permitted.
*   **Physical Posture:** Relaxed, mirroring the individual's posture. Normal eye contact.
*   **Script Constraint:** Prefrontal cortex is online. Choices and logistical planning are biologically permissible. 
*   **Mandatory Script:** Execute the standard MDI Warm Offer. Reference historical context to prove relational continuity. "I know you need Buster to come with you. We have a pod with a crate ready."
*   **Operational Routing:** Proceed to Pod Transition logistics.

---

## 3. The Intake Failure Rule
If an intake offer is made while an individual is in State 1 or State 2, the offer is structurally invalid. A refusal recorded in State 1 or State 2 is an autonomic defense response, not a cognitive refusal of housing. The offer must be withheld or withdrawn until State 3 is achieved through co-regulation.

## 4. Neurobiological Rationale (Allostatic Load & PFC Atrophy)

The execution protocols in this matrix are governed by the physical laws of neurobiology under extreme environmental stress:
*   **Cortisol and Catecholamine Flooding:** Chronic exposure to freezing temperatures, physical vulnerability, and sleep deprivation triggers sustained HPA axis activation. This floods prefrontal networks with neurohormones, causing dendritic atrophy and structural degradation in the prefrontal cortex (PFC).
*   **Deactivation of Deliberative Function:** Because the PFC is responsible for executive control, working memory, and rational planning, its physical degradation takes the deliberative systems offline.
*   **Involuntary Defensive Reflexes:** In State 1 (Dorsal Vagal) and State 2 (Sympathetic), the brainstem and autonomic nervous system govern behavioral output. The refusal of housing or treatment recorded in these states represents an involuntary survival reflex rather than a considered preference. Providing a Warm Offer or demanding compliance during these states is a neurological category error that leads directly to system failure.
