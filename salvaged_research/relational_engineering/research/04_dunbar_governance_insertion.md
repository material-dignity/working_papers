## Supplemental Draft Insertion: Relational Governance and the Capability Bridge

**Target: WP5 (Relational Engineering)**

### The Sequential Resolution of Capability
MDI structures its residential operations to resolve the philosophical tension between Martha Nussbaum's universal thresholds and Amartya Sen's democratic agency through a strict chronological sequence:

1.  **The Threshold Phase (Phase Zero)**: The system secures biological stability, sanitation, and physical safety through clinical environment design. In this acute phase, choice is secondary to physiological survival, guaranteeing Nussbaum's basic physical thresholds (Life, Bodily Health, and Bodily Integrity).
2.  **The Agency Phase (Post-Stabilization)**: Once biological baselines are restored, the individual transitions to a self-governed Dunbar Pod. This transition marks the shift from clinical containment to Sen's open-ended agency.

### Dunbar Pod Councils
Human beings possess cognitive limits regarding the size of stable groups they can navigate. MDI structures its residential floors using Dunbar’s Number. Units are grouped into clusters of sixteen or fewer. By limiting co-located groups, the architecture reduces social friction and prevents the chaotic dynamics of traditional congregate shelters. Within these micro-communities, residents regain agency by casting votes in the Pod Council, utilizing their stabilized "internal conversion factors" to steer their own social and environmental outcomes.
