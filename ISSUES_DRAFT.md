### Issue: Cooperative enterprise revenue projections at 50, 100, and 150 resident enrollment

## Implementation and Case Studies

---

### Issue: Existing cooperative housing model analogues
**Labels:** `implementation`, `help wanted`

**Description:**
We need documented case studies of existing cooperative housing models that approximate the MDI Cooperative Reintegration Mechanism architecture, whether domestic or international. Priority targets include Italian Type B social cooperatives, UK social enterprises operating in supportive housing, and any cooperative model that combines resident-worker ownership with clinical support services. Each case study should document governance structure, revenue model, resident outcomes, and operational scale.

*Researchers or practitioners with direct knowledge of cooperative housing implementations are needed.*

---

### Issue: IPS integration case studies within residential settings
**Labels:** `implementation`, `help wanted`

**Description:**
The Individual Placement and Support (IPS) model is integrated directly within the MDI cooperative ownership structure. We need case studies documenting IPS implementation within residential or congregate settings specifically, covering placement rates, employment retention at 12 and 24 months, and any documented interaction between IPS participation and housing retention outcomes.

*Employment services researchers or IPS practitioners with residential program experience are needed.*

---

### Issue: Pod Steward role analogues in existing peer support models
**Labels:** `implementation`, `help wanted`

**Description:**
The Pod Steward is specified as a live-in peer providing continuous ambient relational presence, compensated through Sovereign Residency rather than payroll wage, and excluded from clinical and security functions. We need documented analogues from existing peer support programs, therapeutic community models, or the French Gardien tradition that approximate this role specification. Each analogue should document the role boundary between peer presence and clinical function, the compensation model, and any measured effect on resident retention.

*Clinicians, peer support program managers, or therapeutic community researchers are needed.*

---

### Issue: Transition outcome data from supportive housing to independent tenancy
**Labels:** `implementation`, `help wanted`

**Description:**
The Tenancy Bridge Guarantee is designed to prevent Permanent Institutionalization Risk by creating a self-executing contractual exit from the MDI tower to sovereign tenancy. We need outcome data from existing programs that bridge supportive housing to independent tenancy, specifically measuring transition success rates, time-to-transition, post-transition housing retention at 12 and 24 months, and re-entry rates.

*Researchers with access to longitudinal supportive housing transition datasets are needed.*

## Clinical and Evaluation Design

---

### Issue: Psychometric instrument selection for Identity Capital measurement
**Labels:** `good first issue`, `clinical design`, `help wanted`

**Description:**
The framework requires validated methods for measuring Identity Capital accumulation over a 24-to-36 month pilot. The measurement must capture the three components specified in the glossary: self-efficacy, coherent professional narrative, and agentic capacity. The instrument selection must also align with the two operational RDI metrics already specified: the Named Peer Contact Index (three named reciprocal peer contacts by day 90) and the Steward Recognition Index (90% name recognition by Pod Steward). We need a selection and validation plan for psychometric instruments capable of tracking these constructs longitudinally.

*Researchers with longitudinal evaluation design experience or clinical backgrounds are needed to define these measurement tools.*

---

### Issue: Longitudinal evaluation design for a 24-to-36 month pilot
**Labels:** `clinical design`, `help wanted`

**Description:**
The Singular Prototype Threshold requires all eight binary verification metrics to pass at One California Plaza before network expansion. We need a complete longitudinal evaluation design for a 24-to-36 month pilot covering sampling strategy, control group specification (or justification for its absence), data collection cadence aligned to the Day-30 Functional Indicators and the Phase Zero clearance rate (Verification Metric 7), and the statistical framework for attributing retention gains to the Metabolic Stabilization sequencing design.

*Evaluation researchers with experience in supportive housing pilot design are needed.*

---

### Issue: Leading indicator calibration for 36-month outcome prediction
**Labels:** `clinical design`, `help wanted`

**Description:**
The RDI framework specifies two leading indicators: the Named Peer Contact Index and the Steward Recognition Index. We need calibration analysis determining what thresholds on these 12-month metrics predict 36-month housing retention outcomes. The analysis should identify the minimum Named Peer Contact Index score and minimum Steward Recognition Index score that statistically predict sustained tenancy at 36 months.

*Quantitative researchers with predictive modeling experience in social service outcomes are needed.*

---

### Issue: Return Deficit index construction and measurement methodology
**Labels:** `clinical design`, `help wanted`

**Description:**
The Return Deficit is defined as the aggregate, compounding reduction in labor market returns produced by multiple co-occurring stigmatized institutional markers, where the compound effect is multiplicative rather than additive. We need a formal index construction translating this theoretical construct into a measurable instrument: variable selection, weighting methodology, scoring rubric, and validation protocol against observed employment outcomes in formerly homeless populations.

*Labor economists or quantitative social scientists with stigma measurement experience are needed.*
